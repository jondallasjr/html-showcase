The Writer's Dark Vision

Edgar Allan Poe's deadly prophecy that came true 46 years later

1838: The Fiction

Edgar Allan Poe publishes his only novel, featuring four shipwrecked sailors who draw straws to determine who will be sacrificed. The loser: a cabin boy named Richard Parker.

The Fictional Scene

Four desperate men on a small boat, drawing straws with gaunt, starving faces. The shortest straw determines a terrible fate.

46 YEARS LATER...

1884: The Reality

The yacht Mignonette sinks. Four real survivors find themselves in the same horrific situation as Poe's fiction. They kill and eat their 17-year-old cabin boy to survive. His name was Richard Parker.

Written in the Stars

Mark Twain's cosmic prediction about Halley's Comet

1835: Cosmic Birth

Samuel Clemens (Mark Twain) is born on November 30, 1835, shortly after Halley's Comet appears in the night sky.

1909: The Prediction

"I came in with Halley's Comet and I expect to go out with it. These two unaccountable freaks came in together, they must go out together."

1910: Cosmic Death

Mark Twain dies on April 21, 1910 - exactly one day after Halley's Comet reaches its closest approach to the Sun.

76 YEARS
Halley's Comet orbital period - Twain lived exactly one complete cycle

The Unsinkable Woman

Violet Jessop survived three major maritime disasters

Miss Unsinkable

Ocean liner stewardess Violet Jessop earned her nickname by surviving not one, not two, but three major ship disasters between 1911 and 1916.

1911: RMS Olympic Collision

The Olympic collides with HMS Hawke. Violet helps passengers as the ship lists but stays afloat.

SURVIVOR #1

1912: RMS Titanic Sinking

The "unsinkable" Titanic hits an iceberg. Violet escapes in a lifeboat, watching the great ship sink into the icy Atlantic.

SURVIVOR #2

1916: HMHS Britannic Explosion

The hospital ship Britannic hits a mine and sinks rapidly. Violet jumps into the water and is pulled to safety by rescuers.

SURVIVOR #3

Twice Touched by the Atom

Tsutomu Yamaguchi - the only person to survive both atomic bombs

August 6, 1945: Hiroshima

Yamaguchi sees a B-29 bomber overhead and witnesses the brilliant atomic flash. Burned but alive, he returns home to Nagasaki.

August 9, 1945: Nagasaki

Just three days later, Yamaguchi experiences the second atomic bomb in his hometown. Again, he survives the impossible.

3 DAYS APART

The Only One

Tsutomu Yamaguchi is officially recognized by the Japanese government as the only person to survive both atomic bombings. He lived to age 93, dedicating his later years to peace advocacy.

The Prophecy Plate

Franz Ferdinand's license plate predicted the war's end

June 28, 1914

Archduke Franz Ferdinand's assassination triggers World War I. His car's license plate reads "AIII 118".

AIII 118

The Revelation

A = Armistice
III = 11.11
118 = 1918

The license plate can be read as "Armistice 11.11.18" - the exact date and time the war ended: November 11, 1918 at 11:00 AM.

Eleanor's Grave

The gravestone that inspired a Beatles classic

The Hidden Connection

In St. Peter's churchyard Woolton, just yards from where Paul McCartney met John Lennon in 1957, lies Eleanor Rigby's grave. Nine years later, Paul would write the song that made her name immortal.

1957: The Meeting

16-year-old Paul McCartney meets John Lennon at St. Peter's Church party. The moment that would create the Beatles.

1966: The Song

Paul writes "Eleanor Rigby," later discovering the gravestone just yards from where he and John first met, suggesting a subliminal connection.
