The essentials

Don't Miss

The must-do experiences for your ten days in Amsterdam: art, animals, music, hidden spaces, and the moments that make this city unforgettable.

Amsterdam in late May is close to perfect: tulips still blooming, long golden evenings, and the big crowds still a week or two away.

Art & Dutch Masters

The Rijksmuseum alone could fill two days. The trick is going early, going often.

Rijksmuseum — Gallery of Honour, 9AM with Fast Lane essential
Four Vermeers in one room: the Milkmaid, Woman Reading a Letter, The Love Letter. The Night Watch at the end of the hall. Vrienden membership gets you through the Fast Lane before the crowds arrive. The Gallery of Honour at 9AM is nearly empty. Plan a full morning; the café is excellent for mid-visit coffee.
Rijksmuseum — Art & Dementia Guided Tour
Daily 14:30–16:00. A guided tour designed for visitors with dementia and their companions: calm, participatory, and genuinely moving. €30 for up to 12 people. Book via the Rijksmuseum website. One of the most thoughtful museum programs in Europe.
Van Gogh Museum pre-book required
Walk-in tickets are not available; you must book in advance at vangoghmuseum.nl. Best visited on a weekday morning. The permanent collection follows Van Gogh's complete arc chronologically, and the building itself is exceptional. Children free under 18.
Anne Frank House tickets release Tuesdays 10AM CET
Tickets release six weeks in advance on Tuesdays at exactly 10:00AM CET. For late May, that means mid-to-late March. The annex has steep narrow stairs; no stroller access. Best plan: Jon and Jess take an evening slot (open until 10PM); Scott, Edie, and Bodhi enjoy the Begijnhof or a canal walk. €16.50/adult. Bodhi pays €1 booking fee only.
Also worth it: Our Lord in the Attic — hidden Catholic church

A full Catholic church concealed inside the attic of a 17th-century canal house on Oudezijds Voorburgwal. Built secretly during the Reformation and used for 200 years before Catholics could worship openly. Three floors of canal house below; a full nave above. One of Amsterdam's most extraordinary hidden spaces. See the Churches & Spaces page for details.

Nature & Animals

Bodhi's territory, but Artis and Hortus are genuinely great for everyone.

Keukenhof Gardens closes May 10 — go day 2
Seven million tulip bulbs, flat wide paths, spectacular late-variety blooms, and it's free for Bodhi. This is the most time-critical item on the list: Keukenhof closes May 10, so go on day 2 at the latest. Buy tickets in advance at keukenhof.nl (~€21.50). Take the Keukenhof Express bus from Amsterdam RAI, €18.20 round trip, runs direct.
Artis Royal Zoo top pick for Bodhi
Seven minutes from the apartment on foot. Follow the Artis de Partis toddler route: petting zoo, butterfly pavilion, sensory playground. Go weekday morning to beat school groups. Free for under-3. Allow 2–3 hours. The zoo is small enough to do at a toddler's pace without exhaustion. Bodhi has a Sunflower Lanyard option if crowds are overwhelming.
Hortus Botanicus — butterfly house top pick
Ten-minute walk from the apartment. One of Amsterdam's most manageable attractions: 1.2 hectares, seven greenhouses, and a tropical butterfly house where butterflies land on you. Beautiful for Edie in the morning before the groups arrive. Free for under-6 (Bodhi gets in free). €13.50 for adults, or free with City Card.
Vondelpark — Groot Melkhuis playground free
Fifteen minutes from the apartment. Fenced toddler area with sandpit and baby swings. The adjacent Groot Melkhuis café has wood-fired pizza, poffertjes, and reasonable coffee. This becomes Bodhi's daily backyard. Best in morning before crowds arrive; check if DiscoDip festival has the park on weekends.

Music & Performance

The Concertgebouw is one of the great concert halls of the world. Use it.

Emmylou Harris at the Concertgebouw — May 24 book now
Americana in one of the world's great concert halls, acoustically extraordinary. With Jim Lauderdale. ~€60 per ticket. This sells out. Book immediately at concertgebouw.nl.
Also: Le nozze di Figaro — Dutch National Opera book now

May 23, 25, and 28. New production of Mozart's opera with the Netherlands Chamber Orchestra at the Dutch National Opera, two minutes from the apartment on performance nights. Various price levels by seat. Book at operaballet.nl. See Music & Events for full details.

Concertgebouw Free Wednesday Lunchtime Concert free
May 27, 12:30PM in the Recital Hall. About 30 minutes of live music, free. Arrive 20 minutes early; tickets are limited to 2 per person with a €2.50 transaction fee. Register at concertgebouw.nl when the program is announced.
Vondelpark Openluchttheater free
Outdoor stage in the park, free performances all summer. Season starts Whitsun weekend (May 24). Everything from children's theater to jazz to dance. Check openluchttheater.nl in April for the full program. Perfect for a family afternoon; bring a blanket.

Peaceful Spaces

The quietest and most beautiful places in a busy city.

Begijnhof courtyard free
A 14th-century enclosed garden, 20 minutes from the apartment, with Amsterdam's oldest wooden house (c.1420) still standing inside. Silence is natural here; it's not posted so much as felt. One of the genuinely sacred spots in Amsterdam, and almost no one outside the tourist trail finds it. Enter through the inconspicuous door on Gedempte Begijnensloot.
Canal walks at dawn or dusk free
The Prinsengracht, Keizersgracht, and Herengracht before 8AM are a different city. Houseboats waking up, cyclists, no tour groups. The golden hour light in late May doesn't end until after 9PM. Evening walks along the Amstel or Brouwersgracht are some of the best free experiences Amsterdam offers.
Also: Rijksmuseum gardens free

The formal gardens on either side of the Rijksmuseum tunnel are free to walk through: sculpture, seasonal plantings, and a café. A quiet half-hour between museum visits or a place to let Bodhi run while others rest. See Churches & Spaces for more calm spots.

Essential Amsterdam

The things that are simply Amsterdam: boat, stroopwafel, canal.

Canal cruise do it twice
60–75 minutes, fully seated, covers the full canal ring. Calming for everyone; Bodhi often falls asleep on the gentle motion. Free for under-4. Use your City Card on Stromma for free entry. Blue Boat Company and Lovers are the other main operators. Go once in the afternoon and once at dusk.
Fresh stroopwafel at Albert Cuyp Market €1.50–2
One of the longest outdoor markets in Europe, open Monday–Saturday. The stroopwafels made fresh on the iron (two thin waffles with caramel syrup pressed between them) are so different from the packaged version that it barely counts as the same food. Go mid-morning before the lunch rush. The market is a 20-minute walk from the Vondelpark area.
Miffy Museum (Nijntje Museum), Utrecht — day trip book ahead
Designed specifically for ages 2–6. Twelve rooms to crawl, explore, and play, all built around Dick Bruna's simple, joyful visual language. This is Bodhi's peak age for it. Utrecht is 30 minutes by direct train from Amsterdam Centraal. Book at nijntjemuseum.nl. Free for under-2.

Book these before you leave

Some take minutes. Some require being ready at a specific moment. All are worth doing now.

Anne Frank House — most time-sensitive

Tickets release Tuesdays at exactly 10:00AM CET, six weeks before your visit date. For a late-May visit, that means mid-to-late March. Create your account at annefrank.org now so you're ready the moment tickets open.

The annex has steep narrow stairs; no stroller access. Best plan: Jon and Jess take an evening slot (open until 10PM), Scott, Edie, and Bodhi enjoy the Begijnhof or a canal walk.

Complete booking checklist

  • Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum — buy immediately best value Two duo memberships at €90 each = €180 total for all 4 adults. Digital card emailed right away. Unlocks Fast Lane entry, unlimited visits, 10% café discount, 15% shop discount. Best money you'll spend.
  • Anne Frank House — Tuesdays 10AM CET, 6 weeks out Mid-to-late March. €16.50/adult. Bodhi pays €1 booking fee only. Create account at annefrank.org first.
  • Emmylou Harris, Concertgebouw — May 24 sells out Book now at concertgebouw.nl. ~€60/ticket.
  • Le nozze di Figaro, Dutch National Opera May 23, 25, or 28. Various prices by seat. Book at operaballet.nl.
  • Keukenhof + Keukenhof Express bus keukenhof.nl for park tickets (~€21.50 online). Keukenhof Express bus from Amsterdam RAI = €18.20 round trip per person. Bodhi free on both.
  • Miffy Museum Utrecht (Nijntje Museum) nijntjemuseum.nl. Book in advance. Designed for ages 2–6.
  • I amsterdam City Card 72h €108/person, buy online. Activate Day 4 morning; covers through Day 7 evening.
Rijksmuseum exhibitions closing May 25

"Metamorphoses" and "FAKE!" both close on May 25. If either interests you, plan to visit the Rijksmuseum in your first day or two. The permanent collection (Gallery of Honour, the Vermeers, the Night Watch) is always there.
