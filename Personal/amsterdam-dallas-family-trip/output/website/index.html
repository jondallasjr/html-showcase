Your family's Amsterdam

This isn't a generic travel guide; it's built specifically for the five of you. Jon and Jess are navigating Amsterdam with Bodhi Bear, who'll be nearly two and free at almost everything. Scott, who plays piano and loves art, is in the right city at the right time. And Edie will find that Amsterdam, done thoughtfully, is genuinely calm, manageable, and beautiful.

You're based in Plantage — a quiet, residential neighborhood east of the center. Artis Zoo is a seven-minute walk. Hortus Botanicus is ten minutes. The Dutch National Opera is practically next door. You're not in the tourist scrum, but you're close enough to everything that matters.

This guide covers everything: the one-time experiences worth booking months out, the free daily pleasures, where to eat near the apartment, and how to make the cards work so you're not overpaying. Browse by topic, not by day; the trip will find its own rhythm.

Book these before you leave home
  • Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum — Buy 2 duo memberships (€90 each = €180 for 4 adults) at rijksmuseum.nl. Digital card emailed immediately. This is the single most important purchase.
  • Anne Frank House — Tickets release Tuesdays at 10AM CET, exactly 6 weeks in advance. That means mid-to-late March for your dates. Create your account at annefrank.org now so you're ready to click.
  • Keukenhof — Closes May 10, which is before your trip ends but day 2 works perfectly. Book tickets at keukenhof.nl and the Keukenhof Express bus (€18.20 round trip from Amsterdam RAI).
  • Emmylou Harris at Concertgebouw — May 24. ~€60/ticket. Sells out. Book now at concertgebouw.nl.
  • Miffy Museum, Utrecht — Nijntje Museum, for Bodhi. Book tickets in advance at nijntjemuseum.nl.
  • I amsterdam City Card 72h — €108/person, buy online. Plan to activate Day 4 morning, covering through Day 7 evening.
