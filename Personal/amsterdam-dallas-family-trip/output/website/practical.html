Practical Info

Getting around, with Bodhi

Trams, cargo bikes, contactless payments, Sunflower Lanyards, and everything else that makes the difference between a smooth trip and a stressful one.

Getting around

Amsterdam is compact and very walkable. The tram fills the gaps. You won't need taxis or a car for anything on the itinerary.

Cargo bike (bakfiets)

The local family solution, and the most enjoyable way to see the city. Bodhi rides in the enclosed wooden box up front. Rentals at MacBike or Star Bikes (~€15–20/day standard; cargo bikes ~€25–35/day).

Bikes have right of way over pedestrians. Trams have right of way over bikes: always yield to trams on rail lines.

Tram

GVB operates trams, buses, and metro. Tap any contactless bank card or phone on the yellow reader when you board, then tap again when you exit. No special transit card needed. Day passes available at GVB service points.

Tram 2 and Tram 11 are most useful: they link the Vondelpark area directly to Amsterdam Centraal.

Walking

Most sites in Oud-Zuid, De Pijp, and Plantage are within 30 minutes of each other on foot. The core neighbourhood is very stroller-friendly; most routes are smooth despite occasional cobblestones.

The Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, and Vondelpark form one easy walkable cluster from the apartment.

Taxi & rideshare

Uber and Bolt both work in Amsterdam. Taxis are available but expensive. Use for airport transfers or late-night returns only. No need for a car rental at any point during the trip.

Key tram routes from Oud-Zuid / Vondelpark

  • 2
    Vondelpark area → Amsterdam Centraal Direct line from the neighbourhood to the main train station. Useful for canal cruise departures and heading north into the city.
  • 11
    Vondelpark ↔ Centraal via Leidseplein Passes Leidseplein, useful for the Concertgebouw area and evening destinations. Runs frequently.
  • 3
    Museumplein → Dam Square → Centraal Use after Rijksmuseum or Van Gogh visits to get to the historic centre or station.
  • 24
    De Pijp / Albert Cuyp Market → Centraal North-south through the city. Handy for the Albert Cuyp Market and heading toward the waterfront.
Schiphol to Amsterdam Centraal

Direct train, 15–20 minutes, runs every 10 minutes, €5.60/person. First class upgrades available. Easiest airport-to-city connection in Europe. Follow train signs directly from baggage claim. From Centraal, take a tram to the apartment.

Canal boats run throughout the day — covered boats are available if it rains. Strollers fold into boat storage.

Travelling with Bodhi

Amsterdam is genuinely toddler-friendly. Here's everything that's specific to the trip with a young child.

Stroller

The city is stroller-accessible. Most trams have low-floor boarding and dedicated stroller space; look for the baby/wheelchair symbol near the doors. Canal boats have storage for folded strollers.

Heavier-wheeled strollers handle cobblestones better than lightweight umbrella folds. Bring a cable lock to secure it outside small shops. Avoid the narrowest canal paths, as some have steep drop-offs with no railing.

Baby & toddler supplies
  • Albert Heijn — main supermarket chain, stocks Pampers, formula, baby food, wet wipes. Open daily until at least 10 PM. Multiple branches near the apartment.
  • Kruidvat — pharmacy/drugstore, nappies, cream, OTC medicines. Cheaper than the supermarket for consumables.
Sunflower Lanyard

The Sunflower Lanyard signals hidden disabilities to trained staff; they offer quiet, unhurried assistance without needing an explanation. Available free from the Amsterdam Centraal info desk and at the entrance of most major attractions.

Participating venues include Artis Zoo, Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, and Anne Frank House. Staff are trained to recognise it and respond discreetly.

Nap logistics

Plan for one daily nap, whether at the apartment, in the stroller, or on a canal boat cruise. The gentle motion of canal boats often sends young children to sleep, which makes the 75-minute cruise a good afternoon slot.

Most large museums (Rijksmuseum, Artis, Van Gogh) have baby change rooms. For cafés, it's always worth asking at the bar.

Nearest pharmacy (apotheek)

Apotheek on Overtoom, a short walk from the Vondelpark apartment. Open weekdays 8:30 AM–5:30 PM, Saturday mornings. Out-of-hours pharmacies operate on a rota; the nearest open one is posted in pharmacy windows or at apotheek.nl. Paracetamol and ibuprofen are also available in all supermarkets.

Money & payments

The Netherlands is almost entirely cashless. Your contactless card or phone works for trams, restaurants, most market stalls, and everywhere else.

Contactless everywhere

Visa, Mastercard, Apple Pay, and Google Pay accepted almost universally, including trams, ferries, and most street stalls. It's genuinely rare to need cash. Don't stress about it.

ATMs if you need cash

ING and ABN AMRO bank ATMs accept most foreign cards at reasonable fees. Avoid GWK Travelex exchange offices near tourist areas. Poor rates and high fees. Your bank's network beats them.

Tipping

Not expected in the Netherlands. Rounding up or leaving 5–10% is appreciated if service was excellent. Cafés and bars typically expect nothing.

VAT refund (non-EU shoppers)

Available on purchases over €50 at participating shops. Ask for a Tax-Free shopping form at the till and process it at Schiphol on departure.

Weather & packing

15–19°C

Late May daytime. Evenings drop to 9–13°C. Rain can arrive at any time but rarely lasts all day. Sun sets around 9:30 PM. Long, beautiful evenings for outdoor walks and parks.

Pack list

  • Waterproof jacket — non-negotiable for every family member including Bodhi
  • Layers — three thin layers beat one thick one for changing conditions
  • Comfortable walking shoes that handle wet cobblestones without slipping
  • One smart outfit for the Concertgebouw or Dutch National Opera
  • Compact umbrella — wind makes full-size umbrellas awkward
  • Sunscreen — late May sun gets strong when the cloud lifts
  • Reusable water bottle — tap water is excellent throughout the Netherlands
  • EU plug adapter (Type C/F round pins, 230V)

For Bodhi specifically

  • Rain cover for the stroller
  • Warm layer for canal boat trips (it's colder on the water)
  • Sun hat for Keukenhof and park days
  • Puddle suit if you have one
  • Small day pack the adults take turns carrying

Plug note: US devices need a Type C/F adapter and should be checked for voltage compatibility (look for 100–240V on the charger label). Buy a multi-socket EU adapter before you leave. Leidseplein area shops also stock them.

Emergency & health

Emergency contacts

112
All emergencies — police, fire, ambulance. Works from any mobile, no SIM required.
0900-8844
Non-emergency police — lost property, non-urgent incidents.
OLVG
Nearest hospital with A&E — OLVG Oosterpark, Oosterpark 9. Approximately 2 km from the Vondelpark apartment. Full emergency department.
AMC Emma
Paediatric emergencies — Emma Children's Hospital, Meibergdreef 9. Amsterdam's specialist children's hospital, about 20 minutes by taxi.
Travel insurance

Carry your EHIC (European Health Insurance Card) or private travel insurance card. Medical costs are billed upfront; keep all receipts to claim back. Ensure your policy covers all three of you including Bodhi.

Wi-Fi & mobile data

Apartment has Wi-Fi. Free Wi-Fi at most cafés, museums, and Amsterdam Centraal. For mobile data: Lebara or Simyo SIMs from Kruidvat (€15–30). Airalo eSIM works if you prefer to set up before flying.

Language & customs

English everywhere — but a few words help

Everyone in Amsterdam speaks English, including shop assistants, tram drivers, and museum staff. No Dutch required. A few words go a long way:

  • Dank je wel — thank you (pronounced: dank-ye-vell)
  • Alsjeblieft — please / here you go (als-ye-bleeft)
  • Apotheek — pharmacy
  • Albert Heijn — pronounced "albert hine" — the main supermarket chain
Dutch directness

Amsterdammers are famously direct. Not rude — just efficient. "No" means no, "yes" means yes. Refreshing once you adjust to it.

Cycling rules

Stay off bike paths when walking. Cyclists come fast from unexpected directions. Always look both ways before crossing any path; tram rails and cycle lanes often overlap where you don't expect them.

Quiet hours

Generally observed after 10 PM in residential areas. The neighbourhood around the apartment is residential, worth knowing if Bodhi has a late bedtime routine.
