Best Deals

Two cards, a clear activation strategy, and Bodhi gets into almost everything free.

The two cards you need

The Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum membership and the I amsterdam City Card 72h are designed differently and complement each other well. Here's exactly how to use them.

Most important purchase

Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum

€180 for 4 adults (2 duo memberships)

Each "Vrienden" membership is €90 and covers 2 people. Buy two and all four adults are covered. Digital cards are emailed immediately; no waiting for the post. Buy at rijksmuseum.nl/en/friends before the trip.

  • Unlimited free entry to the Rijksmuseum — every visit, no booking fee
  • Fast Lane priority entry — no queuing, ever. Enormous on busy days.
  • 10% off at the Rijksmuseum café (useful for a long day)
  • 15% off at the Rijksmuseum shop
  • Access to the Rijksmuseum garden (free anyway, but included)

Break-even point: Regular admission is €22.50/adult. Each duo membership pays for itself after 2 visits per person. You'll easily do 3–4 Rijksmuseum visits in 10 days; the Art & Dementia tour alone is one, and you'll want to go back. This card pays for itself.

Bodhi is free under 18 at the Rijksmuseum — always.
Activate Day 4 morning

I amsterdam City Card 72h

€432 for 4 adults (€108/person)

Buy online at iamsterdam.com before the trip. Activate it on Day 4 morning; this gives you coverage from Day 4 through Day 7 evening, hitting the busiest activity days in the middle of the trip.

  • Artis Royal Zoo (€26.50/adult otherwise)
  • Hortus Botanicus (€13.50/adult otherwise)
  • Stedelijk Museum (modern art)
  • NEMO Science Museum
  • Moco Museum (Banksy + modern art)
  • Rembrandt House
  • Maritime Museum
  • 1 free canal cruise (Stromma)
  • Unlimited GVB public transport (trams, buses, metros) for 72 hours
  • 24h bike rental included
  • Does NOT include Anne Frank House
  • Does NOT include Van Gogh Museum
  • Does NOT include Royal Palace on Dam Square

For Artis + Hortus alone: €26.50 + €13.50 = €40/adult × 4 adults = €160. Add the Stedelijk, Moco, NEMO, and a canal cruise, and the card earns its keep easily. Schedule your Artis and Hortus visits during the City Card window.

Free attractions

No card needed. Some of the best things in Amsterdam cost nothing.

Begijnhof

Medieval courtyard. Most peaceful spot in the city. Open daily 10–18.

Rijksmuseum gardens

Open to anyone. Beautiful in May. Benches, roses, sculpture.

Vondelpark

Six playgrounds, open lawns, ducks, free outdoor theatre in May.

Concertgebouw Wed lunchtime

May 27, 12:30PM. ~30 min in the Recital Hall. Max 2 tickets, €2.50 fee only. Arrive early.

NEMO rooftop

The roof terrace is free to access: water features and views over the harbor.

Free ferry to Noord

From Centraal Station, 5 min across the IJ. Great for NDSM flea market (May 30–31).

Amsterdam Art Week galleries

May 19–24 — galleries citywide open free during Art Week.

Vondelpark Openluchttheater

Free outdoor performances. Season opens Whitsun weekend (May 24).

Westerkerk

Free church entry. Rembrandt is buried here. Next to Anne Frank House.

Day-to-day transport

OVpay — easiest option

Tap any contactless Visa or Mastercard directly on the tram/bus card reader. No OV-chipkaart needed. Single trip: €3.40. Daily cap: ~€10.50 per person.

Use OVpay for any travel outside your City Card 72h window. Bodhi rides free. No setup required; it just works with your existing bank card.

Grocery strategy

SPAR is 5 minutes from the apartment on Nieuwe Kerkstraat; pick up daily supplies on the way home. Albert Heijn Museumplein (Van Baerlestraat 33a, underground entrance, 8AM–10PM) is worth a visit for a larger shop when you're near Vondelpark. Cook breakfasts and some dinners at the apartment; it makes a real difference to the overall budget.

For baby supplies (nappies, formula, wipes), Etos and Kruidvat are on the main shopping streets. Babonbo (babonbo.com) delivers rental equipment (cots, strollers, car seats) directly to your apartment.

General booking tips
  • Book online, not at the door. Van Gogh Museum, Keukenhof, NEMO — all cheaper online, and some require timed-entry booking anyway.
  • Go at 9AM. Museums open at 9 and the first hour is dramatically quieter, especially with children.
  • Van Gogh Museum is not on the City Card — book separately, ideally outside the 72h window to maximize card value.
  • Concertgebouw free concert — max 2 tickets per person. €2.50 transaction fee each. Program announced one week before. Arrive 20 minutes early.
