# Creative Brief: Amsterdam Dallas Family Trip

## Project Goal
A visual lookbook website for the Dallas family's 10-day Amsterdam trip (May 23 – June 1, 2026). The site helps the family browse what's available — museums, parks, music, food, markets, churches, kid spots — so they can pick what appeals on any given day. No daily itinerary. Just beautiful images and concise, practical descriptions of everything worth checking out.

## Style Guide
`style-guides/website.md` — multi-page, hero images, honest tone, real photography, self-contained HTML.

## Audience
The Dallas family: Jon (dad, musician, online worker), Jess (mom, Zimbabwean), Bodhi Bear (nearly 2), Scott (Jon's dad, 69, pianist/bassist, art and music lover, Christian), and Edie (Jon's stepmom, needs gentle pacing and calm environments). This is a reunion trip — Scott and Edie haven't seen Bodhi in over a year.

## Tone & Voice
- Like a friend who lives in Amsterdam made this for you
- Casual, specific, honest about what's worth it and what's skippable
- No travel-blog fluff, no "hidden gem!!!" energy
- Real prices, real tips, real talk
- Don't mention Alzheimer's by name — just frame things as "gentle," "calm," "good for naps and rest," or note accessibility features naturally
- Don't offer a day-by-day itinerary — the family will decide each morning what they feel like

## Family Context (for writing tone, not to be stated explicitly)
- Bodhi is free at every attraction in Amsterdam (under 2-4 everywhere)
- Edie is best in mornings, needs calm environments, benefits from sensory-rich but not overwhelming experiences. Sundowning in late afternoon.
- Scott is the art/music enthusiast — Vermeer, Anne Frank, classical concerts, churches, maybe a jam session
- Jon and Jess are musicians too — live music outdoors is ideal with Bodhi
- The whole family is vegetarian (Scott/Edie flex with occasional meat)
- They like good deals but understand Amsterdam costs money — focus on best value

## Accommodation
Amsterdam Wittenberg by Cove — serviced apartments in the Plantage neighborhood. Nieuwe Kerkstraat 159. Walking distance to Artis Zoo (400m), Hortus Botanicus, Carré Theatre, Dutch National Opera, Waterlooplein. ~15-20 min walk to Rijksmuseum/Vondelpark, ~20 min to Jordaan/Anne Frank House.

## Visual Design

### Color Palette
- Primary: Deep canal blue (#1a3a5c)
- Secondary: Dutch orange accent (#e87722)
- Background: Warm white (#faf8f5)
- Text: Dark slate (#2c2c2c)
- Accent: Spring green (#4a8c5c) for parks/nature sections

### Typography
- Headings: Playfair Display (serif, elegant but warm)
- Body: Source Sans 3 (clean, readable)
- 18px base, generous line height

### Layout
- Multi-page with shared sticky navigation
- Hero images on each page (full-bleed)
- Card grids for browsable items within categories
- Each card: image, name, 2-3 sentence description, key details (price, distance from apartment, hours)
- Pull quotes for tips and highlights
- `<details>` for deeper info (full opening hours, detailed directions, etc.)
- Tables for price comparisons

### Image Strategy
- Real photography of Amsterdam in spring — canals, tulips, museums, parks, markets
- Specific venues: Rijksmuseum interior, Vondelpark playground, Artis Zoo, Concertgebouw, canal boats, churches
- Mix of wide establishing shots and intimate details
- People in photos — families, musicians, market vendors
- Search for specific venues/places first, generic Amsterdam only for hero/atmosphere shots

## Technical
- Static HTML files, inline CSS, no build system
- Google Fonts (Playfair Display, Source Sans 3)
- CDN icon library (Lucide)
- Responsive, mobile-first (this will be used on phones during the trip)
- Hamburger menu on mobile
- Smooth scroll, hover effects on cards
- All images via relative paths from `images/final/`

## Key Reminders
- No daily itinerary — this is a browse-and-pick lookbook
- No mention of Alzheimer's — just natural framing of calm/gentle options
- Bodhi is free everywhere — mention this naturally, don't make a big deal
- Value focus — best deals, free things, card strategies
- The reunion is the heart of this trip — the city is the backdrop, not the point
- Mobile-first — they'll be looking at this on their phones walking around Amsterdam
