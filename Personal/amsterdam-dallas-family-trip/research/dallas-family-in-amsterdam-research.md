# Amsterdam in May with toddler, Alzheimer's, and Vermeer

**Your 10-day Amsterdam trip is remarkably well-suited to the city's strengths.** Amsterdam in May offers long daylight (sunrise ~5:30 AM, sunset ~9:30 PM), spring flowers still in bloom, and a compact walkable center that minimizes transport stress. The Museumplein/Vondelpark location puts you within walking distance of most key attractions, and nearly every venue in the city admits children under 2–4 for free. The biggest planning priorities: book Keukenhof for the first days of your trip (it closes May 10, 2026), secure Anne Frank House tickets exactly 6 weeks in advance, and purchase two "Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum" duo memberships — the Fast Lane access alone is worth it for a family managing a toddler and Alzheimer's.

## Part A: The smartest combination of discount cards saves roughly €200

### Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum — the clear winner

This is the single best investment for your trip. At **€90 for two people** (annual membership), two duo memberships cover all four adults for **€180 total**. What you get:

- **Unlimited entry** to the Rijksmuseum and all special exhibitions for a full year
- **Friends Fast Lane** — skip the regular queue entirely (invaluable with Edie and Bodie)
- **No timed booking required** — show up whenever you want, leave when you want, come back tomorrow
- **15% off** the museum shop and **10% off** the café
- Children under 18 are always free at the Rijksmuseum regardless of membership

The flexibility alone makes this essential. With a toddler whose mood is unpredictable and a family member with Alzheimer's who may need to leave early, the ability to walk in for 30 minutes to see the Vermeers, leave, and return the next day is transformative. Break-even is just **two visits per person** (vs. €25/visit individual tickets). Over 10 days, you'll likely visit 3–5 times. Sign up online at rijksmuseum.nl/en/tickets — digital cards are emailed immediately.

### I amsterdam City Card — worth it for 3 concentrated days

The **72-hour card at €108/person** (€432 for four adults) packs serious value when used strategically over three museum-heavy days. It includes free entry to **70+ museums and attractions** (Artis Zoo, Hortus Botanicus, Stedelijk Museum, NEMO, Moco Museum, Maritime Museum, Rembrandt House, and dozens more), **unlimited GVB public transport**, **one canal cruise**, and a **24-hour bike rental**. It does **not** include the Anne Frank House, Van Gogh Museum, or Royal Palace.

The card activates at first use and runs for exactly 72 hours — so activating Friday morning covers you through Sunday evening. Concentrate your museum-heavy days within this window.

### What to skip

The **Holland Pass is discontinued**. The **Museumkaart** (€75/person) offers only 5 museum visits for tourists and cannot be renewed without a Dutch address — since the Vrienden membership already covers your most-visited museum, the Museumkaart offers marginal additional value. The **Go City Amsterdam Pass** exists but overlaps heavily with the I amsterdam Card at higher prices.

### Recommended total card strategy

| 4× I amsterdam City Card 72h | €432 | 3 packed days: Artis, Hortus, Stedelijk, NEMO, canal cruise, 60+ museums, transport |

Individual tickets for the same attractions would run approximately **€950–1,050**, saving roughly **€200** plus enormous convenience gains. The Vrienden Fast Lane and I amsterdam Card's bundled transport and canal cruise eliminate the nickel-and-dime friction that erodes vacation days.

### Canal cruise options — ideal for both Edie and Bodie

Canal cruises are among the best activities for this family. The gentle motion is calming for Alzheimer's patients and often lulls toddlers to sleep. **Children under 4 ride free** on virtually all operators.

**Best operators for this family:**
- **Stromma** — largest company, departures from right next to the Rijksmuseum (couldn't be more convenient). Standard 1-hour covered cruise ~€16–20. Included with I amsterdam City Card.
- **Blue Boat Company** — departs from Stadhouderskade near Leidseplein (walkable from your area). Six wheelchair-accessible boats with electric platform lifts. Call ahead at +31 20 6791 370 to reserve accessible boats.
- **Captain Jack** — small boats (max 20 people), live narration, unlimited Dutch snacks, family discounts. More intimate experience, ~€25–40/person.
- **Self-drive electric boats** — rent a small boat and cruise at your own pace from Canal Motorboats (from ~€60/hour for the whole family). Maximum flexibility and control.

For Edie specifically, a **private boat charter** (~€300–400 for the group) offers complete control over duration, pace, and stops — worthwhile if her condition makes shared group settings unpredictable. Alternatively, the covered Stromma boats from the Rijksmuseum dock are perfectly calm and convenient.

## Part B: Attractions ranked by family suitability

### The four Vermeers at the Rijksmuseum

The Rijksmuseum permanently displays four Vermeer masterpieces in the **Gallery of Honour on Floor 2**: *The Milkmaid* (c. 1660), *Woman Reading a Letter* (c. 1663), *The Little Street* (c. 1660), and *The Love Letter* (c. 1669–70). These sit alongside Rembrandt's *Night Watch* in the museum's most celebrated galleries. The blockbuster 28-painting Vermeer exhibition ended in 2023, but these four permanent works are extraordinary.

**Best visiting strategy:** Arrive at **9:00 AM on a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday** — the first 30 minutes are described as "magical" with near-empty galleries. The Gallery of Honour is the busiest area by 11 AM. With your Vrienden Fast Lane, you skip the entrance queue entirely. The museum is **fully stroller-accessible** with lifts to all floors, and lends free wheelchairs, rollators, and pushchairs at the information desk. There is a dedicated **quiet room** for visitors who feel overwhelmed, and quieter galleries are marked green on the floorplan.

For Edie specifically, the Rijksmuseum offers **"Art and Dementia" private guided tours** — specially trained guides lead groups of up to 12 people through selected artworks, encouraging stories and memories rather than factual recall. Tours run daily **14:30–16:00** at just **€30 per tour** (not per person). Participants receive folding stools. Book via rijksmuseum.nl/en/whats-on/accessibility/art-and-dementia. Museum entry is separate.

### Anne Frank House requires precise planning

Tickets go on sale **every Tuesday at 10:00 AM CET**, covering dates **6 weeks later**. For a May visit, you need to be online in **mid-to-late March 2026**. Create your account at annefrank.org in advance, log in 15 minutes before release, and have payment ready. May is peak season — tickets can sell out within hours. There is no waiting list, but occasional cancellations appear later.

**Critical family considerations:** The Anne Frank House has **steep, narrow stairs** throughout the historical annex. It is **not stroller-accessible** (strollers stored in cloakroom), **not wheelchair-accessible** in the annex sections, and the experience is emotionally intense. **This is almost certainly not appropriate for Edie** given both the physical demands and the emotional weight. It is technically open to all ages including toddlers (€1 booking fee for under-10s), but the content and environment aren't suited to a nearly-2-year-old either.

**Recommended approach:** John and Jess visit the Anne Frank House during an **evening slot** (the house is open until 10 PM April–August, and evening slots are less crowded), while Scott stays with Edie and Bodie at the nearby Jordaan canal-side cafés or Vondelpark. Tickets are **€16.50/adult**.

### Keukenhof closes May 10 — visit immediately

Keukenhof Gardens opens **March 19 through May 10, 2026**. If you arrive in early May, you have a narrow window. Late-season tulips are still gorgeous, with late-variety blooms and larger flowers. The gardens are **fully stroller-accessible** with flat, paved paths, ample benches, and multiple cafés. Children under 4 enter free. Adult tickets are **~€21.50 online** (€25 at the door).

**Getting there:** The **Keukenhof Express bus from Amsterdam RAI station** is the most practical option — round-trip €18.20/adult, free for under-4. Total journey ~45 minutes each way. Alternatively, combi-tickets (entrance + bus) run ~€38.50/adult from Amsterdam.

**For Edie:** Keukenhof is sensory-rich but manageable — flowers, fresh air, gentle walking paths. It also offers **"Whisper Boats"** — quiet electric boats through surrounding canals, sold separately. Visit on a **weekday morning before 10:30 AM** to avoid crowds. Monday through Wednesday are quietest. Allow 3–4 hours for a comfortable visit.

### Vondelpark is your backyard and daily anchor

With six play areas, multiple cafés, and an open-air theatre, Vondelpark will become your family's daily rhythm. Key spots:

- **Groot Melkhuis** — the family headquarters. Huge fenced playground with separate toddler area, sandpit, baby swings, and a café serving wood-fired pizza and poffertjes. Bouncy castle on sunny weekends.
- **De Vondeltuin** — at the park's southern tip, a nature-style playground with giant climbing structure, massive slide, and sandbox. Adjacent family café with kids' menu.
- **Paddling pool** — opens in warm weather. Perfect for a nearly-2-year-old on May's warmer days.
- **Openluchttheater** — the open-air theatre's season begins in May with free performances including classical music, comedy, and children's shows. Check openluchttheater.nl.

### Other standout attractions for this family

**Artis Royal Zoo** is a toddler paradise. The dedicated "Artis de Partis" route is designed for young children, with a petting zoo, butterfly pavilion, sensational playground, and free trolleys for tired legs. Under-3s enter free; adults pay **€26.50 online**. The zoo is included in the I amsterdam City Card. It's located in the quiet Plantage neighborhood with wide, flat paths — calm enough for Edie on weekday mornings. ARTIS recognizes the **Hidden Disabilities Sunflower lanyard** and has spare lanyards at the ticket desk.

**Hortus Botanicus** is possibly the single best attraction for Edie — tranquil, sensory-rich, and small enough to feel manageable (1.2 hectares). Founded in 1638, it features seven greenhouses including a butterfly house, exotic plants, and the lovely Orangery café. Adults ~€13.50, free for under-6, included with I amsterdam City Card. Visit duration is a comfortable 1–2 hours.

**Begijnhof** is free, supremely peaceful, and ideal for Edie — an enclosed 14th-century courtyard where visitors are asked to respect silence. See Amsterdam's oldest wooden house (c. 1420). Open daily 10:00–18:00 (closed Sundays). Visit in 30–60 minutes.

**Amsterdamse Bos** (Amsterdam Forest) offers a full half-day escape: 1,000 hectares of nature, free entry, toddler paddling pools (open May–September), the **Ridammerhoeve goat farm** with 150+ goats and pancakes, and the Boerderij Meerzicht pancake house with playground, deer, and peacocks. Heritage trams run on Sundays. Reachable by bus or bike (~30 min from center).

**Amstelpark** is a hidden gem with Amsterdam's largest outdoor playground, a mini train ride (~€3), petting zoo, hedge maze, mini golf, and pancake house — all essentially free beyond the train.

**Miffy Museum in Utrecht** is a 30-minute train ride away and designed specifically for ages 2–6. Twelve thematic rooms where toddlers crawl, climb, and play — Miffy's house, a traffic village with tricycles, a doctor's office, farm, and zoo room. **Tickets sell out — book well in advance.** A wonderful half-day trip.

### May events worth catching

- **Remembrance Day (May 4):** The city observes two minutes of silence at 8 PM at Dam Square — a moving experience.
- **Liberation Day (May 5):** Festivals across the city, including the family-friendly **Vrijheidsplein** area at the Westerpark festival and a free concert on the Amstel.
- **Rolling Kitchens (mid-May, dates TBC):** Massive food truck festival in Westerpark — casual outdoor dining, very family-friendly.
- **Vondelpark Open Air Theatre:** Free performances begin in May.
- **Concertgebouw free Wednesday lunchtime concerts:** Classical music at 12:30 PM in the Kleine Zaal — calm, beautiful, and free. Right across from Museumplein.

## Part C: Making Amsterdam work for Edie

### The Rijksmuseum's dementia program is world-class

The museum holds **"Samen Dementievriendelijk"** (Together Dementia-Friendly) certification. Beyond the Art and Dementia tours described above, staff are trained to recognize the **Hidden Disabilities Sunflower lanyard/keycord** — wearing one signals to staff that Edie may need extra patience and assistance without requiring explanation. The museum offers a **Fast Lane Declaration** for visitors who cannot wait in queues (no proof of condition required), a **free companion admission** for visitors who cannot navigate independently, and downloadable preparatory documents to help Edie know what to expect before arriving.

### The Sunflower Lanyard works across Amsterdam

The **Hidden Disabilities Sunflower** scheme is active throughout the Netherlands. **Schiphol Airport** recognizes it (staff provide extra time at security), as do the Rijksmuseum, ARTIS Zoo (spare lanyards available at ticket desks), Dutch supermarkets (Plus, Deka Markt, Lidl), and transport companies. Purchase from hdsunflower.com/nl/ or obtain free at participating venues. This is a simple, powerful tool.

### Safety near Amsterdam's unfenced canals

**Amsterdam's canals have no railings or fences in most areas** — this is the single biggest safety concern for someone who may wander. Always position a family member between Edie and the canal edge when walking. Consider an **Apple AirTag or GPS tracker** attached to Edie's clothing or shoes. Carry a card with the Airbnb address in both Dutch and English, and take a photo of Edie each morning noting what she's wearing.

### The optimal daily rhythm for Edie

People with Alzheimer's tend to be calmest and most alert in the morning, with confusion and anxiety often increasing in late afternoon ("sundowning"). Structure days accordingly:

- **Morning (9:00–12:00):** One calm activity — museum visit, garden walk, or canal cruise
- **Midday (12:00–14:00):** Quiet lunch, return to Airbnb for rest
- **Afternoon (14:00–16:00):** Optional light activity (park bench, short walk) or continued rest
- **Late afternoon/evening:** Return to Airbnb before sundowning. Consider early dinners (17:30–18:00)

**Bring from home:** Noise-canceling headphones (for airports and busy streets), sunglasses (reduce visual overstimulation), familiar comfort items (favorite snack, music), and a medical ID bracelet with Edie's name, "Alzheimer's disease," emergency contact number, and critical medications.

### Best calm spaces when Edie needs a break

The Rijksmuseum has a designated **quiet room**. The **OBA public library** near Centraal Station has panoramic calm reading areas and a rooftop café. **Begijnhof** is an oasis of silence. The **Concertgebouw's free Wednesday concerts** offer structured calm. Canal-side benches along **Reguliersgracht** (one of Amsterdam's most peaceful canals) and **Herengracht** provide outdoor retreats. The Rijksmuseum's own gardens on Museumplein are free, beautiful, and peaceful.

**Emergency contacts:** 112 for emergencies. OLVG Hospital (Oosterpark 9) and Amsterdam UMC have English-speaking staff. US Consulate General: +31 20 592 9031.

## Part D: Amsterdam is free for Bodie — and wonderful for toddlers

Bodie Bear will pay **€0 at every attraction** in Amsterdam. The Rijksmuseum (free under 18), Anne Frank House (€1 booking fee under 10), Artis Zoo (free under 3), NEMO (free under 4), Hortus Botanicus (free under 6), Keukenhof (free under 4), all canal cruises (free under 4), Van Gogh Museum (free under 18), and GVB public transport (free under 4) all welcome toddlers without charge.

### Playgrounds near your Airbnb

The **Groot Melkhuis playground** in Vondelpark is your daily go-to — a large fenced area with a separate toddler section including sandpit, baby swings, and water play. The adjacent café serves pancakes and poffertjes with a terrace overlooking the pond. **De Vondeltuin** at the park's southern end has a nature-style playground with a giant slide and sandbox. **Amstelpark** (further south) has Amsterdam's largest outdoor playground plus a mini train, petting zoo, and hedge maze. **Beatrixpark** nearby has a children's playground and paddling pool. For rainy days, **Blender** (Ruysdaelstraat 9–11, close to Museumplein) is a kids' concept store/restaurant with a huge play area, and **Kinderkookkafé** near Vondelpark lets toddlers help make pancakes.

### Stroller strategy

Amsterdam is surprisingly stroller-friendly with well-maintained pavements and curb cuts. Bring a **compact foldable travel stroller** (like a Babyzen Yoyo) — sturdy enough for cobblestones but small enough for trams. Red sidewalks are bike paths — stay off them. Strollers are welcome at the Rijksmuseum (loaners available), Artis, NEMO, Keukenhof, and Van Gogh Museum. The Anne Frank House does **not** allow strollers inside (cloakroom storage available). For museums and narrow spaces, complement the stroller with a **front baby carrier**. Bring a small cable lock to secure the stroller outside restaurants.

### Family restaurants near Museumplein

**Groot Melkhuis** (Vondelpark) is the family-dining anchor — wood-fired pizza, pancakes, huge playground right outside. **Dignita** (near Vondelpark) is a social-enterprise café with an amazing brunch and a baby-gated play corner. **Blender** (Oud-Zuid) combines a restaurant with a kids' play area. **Kinderkookkafé** lets kids cook their own meals. For Dutch pancakes, try **The Pancake Bakery** (Prinsengracht 191) or **MOAK Pancakes** (spacious, customizable kids' options). **Foodhallen** in De Hallen offers diverse cuisine in an indoor food-hall format — family-friendly but can get crowded on weekends.

### Baby equipment and supplies

**Albert Heijn at Museumplein** (Van Baerlestraat 33a, underground near the Concertgebouw) carries diapers, formula, baby food pouches, and snacks. Open 8 AM–10 PM daily. **Etos** and **Kruidvat** drugstores are nearby for baby-specific toiletries and basic medications. For equipment rental, **Babonbo** (babonbo.com) delivers strollers, travel cots, car seats, and high chairs directly to your Airbnb. **Wanderlust Mama & Co** (wanderlustmamaandco.com) is Amsterdam-based with a similar range.

## Part E: Getting there and getting around

### Flights from Chiang Mai

No direct flights exist. The best single-stop option is **Chiang Mai → Bangkok → Amsterdam on KLM** (1-hour domestic hop + 11.5-hour direct to Schiphol). **Emirates via Dubai**, **Qatar Airways via Doha**, and **Singapore Airlines via Singapore** all offer excellent family-friendly long-haul service with good Chiang Mai connections. Expect **€800–1,200 per person** round-trip in economy for quality carriers. May is actually one of the cheaper months for this route.

**Toddler seat policy:** If Bodie is under 2 on all flight segments, he can fly as a lap infant at roughly **10% of the adult fare** on international flights. However, holding a nearly-2-year-old for 12+ hours is genuinely difficult — **strongly consider purchasing a separate seat** and bringing an FAA-approved car seat. If Bodie turns 2 during the trip, a separate seat is required for any post-birthday flight segments.

**Book 2–3 months in advance** and check Skyscanner for route comparisons. Booking on Sundays yields 6–13% lower fares according to ARC data.

### Airport to Museumplein

**For arrival** with luggage, a toddler, and two seniors: pre-book a **private transfer or large taxi** (€50–60) for door-to-door service. The **Amsterdam Airport Express Bus 397** is the budget option — direct from Schiphol to Museumplein in 25–35 minutes for €6.50/person (under-4 free), departing every 10 minutes. For departure, Bus 397 works well since you'll be lighter and know the system.

### You probably don't need a transport pass

Staying near Vondelpark/Museumplein, most attractions are **walkable**: Rijksmuseum (5 min), Van Gogh Museum (5 min), Stedelijk (5 min), Concertgebouw (5 min), Leidseplein (10 min), Jordaan (20 min), Anne Frank House (20 min), Albert Cuyp Market (15 min), Begijnhof (20 min). You'll need transport mainly for ARTIS Zoo, NEMO, and Centraal Station area.

Use **OVpay** — tap in and out on trams/buses with any **contactless Visa or Mastercard**. No OV-chipkaart needed. Single trips cost €3.40, daily cap ~€10.50. Children under 4 ride free. The I amsterdam City Card includes 72 hours of unlimited transport during your card-active days.

### Bike rental with child seats

A **cargo bike (bakfiets)** is the best option for Bodie — he sits safely in the front box with seatbelts, visible to the rider. **MacBike** at Overtoom (next to Vondelpark) rents cargo bikes from **€32.95/day** with child seats included free. **Black Bikes** (Constantijn Huygenstraat, near Vondelpark) offers rear child seats and e-cargo bikes across 16 locations. **Important caveat:** Amsterdam cycling traffic is intense. If you're not a confident urban cyclist, stick to Vondelpark paths and quieter areas, or skip cycling entirely. Vondelpark is ideal for safe, relaxed family biking.

### Visa and entry requirements

**US citizens** do not need a visa for stays under 90 days. As of May 2026, ETIAS is not yet required (scheduled for Q4 2026). The **EU Entry/Exit System (EES)** launched in late 2025 — fingerprints and facial photos are taken at the border, but it's automatic and free. Passports must be valid 3+ months beyond departure. If any family members are **Thai citizens**, they need a **Schengen visa** — apply at VFS Global in Bangkok at least 45 days before travel (€90 adults, free for under-6, mandatory travel insurance with €30,000 minimum medical coverage).

### Weather and packing

May in Amsterdam averages **highs of 16–17°C (61–63°F)** and lows of **8–9°C (46–48°F)**, with 12–15 days of some rain (usually light showers). The wind makes it feel cooler than the numbers suggest. Pack **layers** (t-shirts + light sweater + medium jacket), a **compact waterproof jacket** for everyone (wind defeats umbrellas), comfortable walking shoes, sunglasses, and sunscreen. For Bodie: rain cover for stroller and a warm hat for evenings. **The Netherlands uses Type C/F plugs** (European 2-pin round) at 230V — bring adapters.

## Part F: A flexible 10-day framework

This itinerary assumes arrival around **May 1** (adjust dates as needed). Each day has a morning anchor activity and afternoon flexibility. Nothing is rigid — swap days freely based on weather, energy, and mood. The principle: **one main activity before lunch, rest during midday, optional light activity in the afternoon.**

### Day 1 — Arrival and settling in

**Morning/Afternoon:** Arrive at Schiphol, transfer to Airbnb. Unpack, grocery run at Albert Heijn Museumplein (Van Baerlestraat 33a). Everyone is jet-lagged — keep it simple.
**Late afternoon:** First stroll through **Vondelpark** — orient yourselves, find the Groot Melkhuis playground, let Bodie run. Grab dinner at Groot Melkhuis or pick up food from Albert Heijn.
**Evening:** Early bedtime. Rest.

### Day 2 — Keukenhof (time-sensitive: closes May 10)

**Full day:** Depart early for **Keukenhof Gardens**. Take the Keukenhof Express bus from Amsterdam RAI (~45 min). Arrive by 9:00 AM to beat crowds. The gardens are stroller-friendly with flat paths, ample benches, and cafés. Bodie enters free. Try the **Whisper Boats** (quiet electric canal boats within the park) — perfect for Edie. Allow 3–4 hours, leave by early afternoon.
**Afternoon:** Return to Amsterdam, rest at Airbnb. Bodie naps. Light Vondelpark visit if energy allows.
**Tip:** Go on a weekday (Monday–Wednesday are quietest). Early May still has beautiful late-variety tulips.

### Day 3 — Rijksmuseum morning, Vondelpark afternoon

**Morning:** First Rijksmuseum visit. Arrive at **9:00 AM** using your Vrienden Fast Lane. Head straight to the **Gallery of Honour (Floor 2)** for the four Vermeers and the Night Watch. Spend 60–90 minutes — leave before crowds build. The museum lends free pushchairs and wheelchairs at the information desk.
**Midday:** Lunch at the Rijksmuseum Café (10% Vrienden discount) or picnic on Museumplein.
**Afternoon:** Bodie naps in stroller at Vondelpark. When he wakes, Groot Melkhuis playground. Edie rests at the Airbnb or joins for a gentle park walk.

### Day 4 — Artis Zoo (I amsterdam Card Day 1)

**Activate I amsterdam City Cards this morning.** 
**Morning:** **Artis Royal Zoo** — arrive at 9:00 AM opening. Follow the "Artis de Partis" route designed for young children. Petting zoo, butterfly pavilion, playground. Bodie is free. Edie will enjoy the calm, green environment. Budget 2–3 hours.
**Afternoon:** Walk or tram to nearby **Hortus Botanicus** (included with I amsterdam Card). This is ideal for Edie — tranquil, sensory-rich, manageable in 1–2 hours. Butterfly greenhouse is magical. Orangery café for a rest.
**Evening:** Early dinner in the Plantage neighborhood.

### Day 5 — Canal cruise and culture (I amsterdam Card Day 2)

**Morning:** **Canal cruise** — use the free cruise included with the I amsterdam City Card. Depart from the Rijksmuseum dock (Stromma) or Stadhouderskade (Blue Boat). The 60–75 minute ride is ideal for a Bodie nap and calming for Edie. Covered, heated boats.
**Late morning:** **Begijnhof** — free, supremely peaceful. 30-minute visit to this medieval courtyard. Then stroll through the **Jordaan neighborhood** — Brouwersgracht, Egelantiersgracht, hidden hofjes. Coffee at **Winkel 43** (famous apple pie) or **Café Papeneiland** (Amsterdam's oldest café, built 1642).
**Afternoon:** Rest at Airbnb. Optional: **Stedelijk Museum** (I amsterdam Card) for modern art lovers, or simply Vondelpark.

### Day 6 — Anne Frank House (split group) + flex

**Evening (pre-booked):** **John and Jess visit the Anne Frank House** at an evening slot (open until 10 PM). Evening visits are significantly less crowded. Budget 1 hour.
**Morning/Day (for the full group):** Relaxed day. **Albert Cuyp Market** (Mon–Sat, 9 AM–5 PM) — Europe's largest daytime market. Stroopwafels, herring, flowers. Then walk to **Sarphatipark** (2 blocks south) for Bodie's playground time.
**Afternoon:** Bodie naps. Edie rests. Free **Concertgebouw Wednesday lunchtime concert** at 12:30 PM if timing aligns (check schedule). Or use the I amsterdam Card Day 3 to visit **NEMO's free rooftop** (water features for Bodie on warm days) or **Rembrandt House**.

### Day 7 — Rest and nature day

**Morning:** Sleep in. Slow breakfast at the Airbnb. This is the built-in recovery day.
**Late morning:** Gentle walk to **Museumplein** and the **Rijksmuseum Gardens** (free, beautiful in May).
**Afternoon option A:** **Amsterdamse Bos** — take a bus to the Amsterdam Forest. Visit the **Ridammerhoeve goat farm** (Bodie will love the goats) and have pancakes at Boerderij Meerzicht. Toddler paddling pools open in May. Free entry.
**Afternoon option B:** Stay local. Second visit to Vondelpark's De Vondeltuin playground. Ice cream from one of the park cafés.
**Evening:** Cook at the Airbnb or order delivery via Flink.

### Day 8 — Rijksmuseum revisit and Van Gogh Museum

**Morning:** **Rijksmuseum dementia tour for Edie** — book the **14:30–16:00 Art and Dementia tour** (€30 for the group). In the morning, visit the **Van Gogh Museum** (pre-booked timed entry, ~€22/adult, under-18 free). Budget 90 minutes. Alternatively, send two adults to Van Gogh while others revisit favorite Rijksmuseum galleries using the Vrienden cards.
**Afternoon:** Edie's dementia tour at the Rijksmuseum (14:30). Others can explore galleries independently or wait at the café. Folding stools provided.
**Evening:** Dinner at **Foodhallen** (De Hallen, Oud-West) — diverse food stalls, family-friendly atmosphere.

### Day 9 — Day trip or Amsterdam deep-dive

**Option A: Miffy Museum in Utrecht.** 30-minute direct train from Amsterdam Centraal. Designed for ages 2–6 — perfect for Bodie. Twelve thematic rooms for crawling, climbing, and playing. **Book tickets in advance.** Combine with exploring charming Utrecht's canals and cafés. This is a lighter day for Edie (scenic train ride + café sitting while others do the museum).

**Option B: Stay in Amsterdam.** Revisit favorites. **Noordermarkt organic farmers' market** (Saturdays 9 AM–4 PM) in the Jordaan — cheese, bread, prepared food. Walk the **Bloemenmarkt** (floating flower market). Visit any museums missed. Another canal cruise (self-drive electric boat this time, ~€60/hour for the family — maximum flexibility and fun).

**Option C: Full rest day.** Vondelpark, playground, poffertjes, ducks, ice cream. Sometimes the best toddler days have no agenda.

### Day 10 — Last morning and departure

**Morning:** Final Vondelpark visit. Say goodbye to the Groot Melkhuis. A last walk along the canals. If time permits, a quick Rijksmuseum pop-in to see the Vermeers one more time (thank you, Vrienden cards).
**Departure:** Bus 397 to Schiphol (25–35 min) or pre-booked taxi.

### Splitting the group — practical configurations

The family naturally splits into care pairs. Useful configurations:

- **John + Jess** go to Anne Frank House while **Scott + Edie + Bodie** enjoy a canal cruise or Vondelpark
- **One adult + Bodie** at a playground while **three adults** do a focused museum visit
- **Scott + Edie** rest at the Airbnb or a quiet café while **John + Jess + Bodie** do an active outing
- **Edie + one companion** do the Rijksmuseum dementia tour while others visit the Van Gogh Museum

Always ensure Edie has at least one companion, and Bodie has at least one adult. With five people, you have enough flexibility to give both Edie and Bodie what they need while adults get cultural experiences.

## Conclusion

This trip plays to Amsterdam's greatest strength: a compact, walkable, spectacularly beautiful city that moves at whatever pace you set. The **Vrienden van het Rijksmuseum** membership is your single most important purchase — it transforms the Rijksmuseum from a one-shot museum visit into a living room you pop into whenever the mood strikes. **Keukenhof's May 10 closing date** is the trip's one hard deadline. The **Art and Dementia tour** at the Rijksmuseum is a rare gift — few cities offer this level of dementia-specific cultural programming. And Amsterdam in May, with its 16 hours of daylight, blooming gardens, canal-side café terraces, and six Vondelpark playgrounds, might be the single best European city for a family balancing a toddler's unpredictability with a grandmother's need for calm. Book the Anne Frank House tickets on a Tuesday morning in late March, sign up for Vrienden today, and let Amsterdam do the rest.
