Language Patterns That Establish Ownership

The way you communicate either reinforces contractor status or establishes business owner authority. After billing £2,000+ per day across government departments, I've identified specific language patterns that position you as an equal rather than hired help.

The Peer Conversation Framework

  • Use "we" to indicate partnership: "We need to address this transformation"
  • Reference your expertise: "In my experience working with similar organizations..."
  • Make strategic recommendations: "I recommend we approach this differently"
  • Discuss outcomes, not tasks: "The result you'll achieve is..."

Behavioral Positioning Strategies

Your behavior either reinforces authority or undermines it. After working with organizations managing £10+ billion programs, I've learned that executive presence is about control, confidence, and strategic focus.

  • Meeting Dynamics: Enter as consultant solving problems, not employee receiving instructions
  • Communication Rhythm: Respond professionally within 24 hours, not instantly like eager employee
  • Project Ownership: Own your expertise domain while respecting their business domain
  • Pricing Conversations: Explain value, don't apologize for rates

The Authority Communication Framework

Authority in consulting comes from demonstrated expertise, strategic thinking, and confident communication.

1

Opening Authority

Start conversations with strategic context: "Based on my work with similar organizations, the typical challenge you're facing is..."

2

Strategic Questions

Ask questions that demonstrate strategic thinking: "What's the business impact if we don't address this transformation?"

3

Recommendation Framework

Present options with clear recommendations: "I see three strategic approaches. Based on your context, I recommend approach B because..."

4

Outcome Focus

Connect every activity to business results: "This will position your organization to achieve X transformation, which typically generates Y value."

Practical Implementation Strategy

Here's how to make this transformation immediately:

Week 1: Language Audit

Week 2: Positioning Adjustment

Rewrite your proposals using business owner language. Focus on transformations achieved rather than services provided. Replace task lists with outcome descriptions.

Week 3: Communication Patterns

Implement the peer conversation framework. Start making strategic recommendations rather than asking for direction. Reference your expertise in comparable situations.

Week 4: Relationship Dynamics

Begin positioning yourself as strategic advisor rather than task executor. Challenge approaches that won't serve transformation goals. Connect activities to business outcomes.
