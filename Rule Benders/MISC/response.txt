            <section class="content-section" id="section-3">
                <h2>The Mozilla RRA Framework: Your 30-Minute Competitive Advantage</h2>
                <img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-9_NvPQTyPw/951f41332c12912623a5ab31e09d0e86ab03eee1663ab5cb6a28afbd83c66f33524f758d96e1b4b0e18ef48fad721ea59cc4c91f44db00411c72a5753c74810b086ba29d85107dd85cbcc2147f6f8f7cb32ef6dd6bf391aa17e8ee29eb043de81874debb" alt="Risk Probability Calculator Tool" class="section-image">
                
                <p>The Rapid Risk Assessment (RRA) methodology I use comes from Mozilla's security team, but I've adapted it for management consulting across government and private sector projects. The beauty of this framework is its speed and reproducibility—characteristics that make it perfect for high-stakes consulting environments.</p>

                <p>The core methodology focuses on three critical questions that cut through complexity:</p>

                <h3><img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-9O02X31Qtw/ff6232467582271bdd2bd6d3c130316b2ee15df3b1db4d0f5c7bda4a2fb8ea3be9660df42e3b5f94dc6d272af20ba99c3606e25190fcc652e3efbc1d5c63ed4d66e83ce1e8234f6af20cbb180df1543ba66d693ac279268b4a5ceb2443acd1a4e3d677bd" class="icon-inline"> Question 1: What happens if all the data/information is disclosed?</h3>
                
                <p>In government consulting, this often means classified information, competitive intelligence, or sensitive commercial data. But the principle applies everywhere. When I'm assessing a client's digital transformation project, I'm asking: what happens if competitors see our strategy, if customers discover our problems, if regulators access our internal processes?</p>

                <h3>Question 2: What happens if the data/process is incorrect or misleading?</h3>
                
                <p>This is where most consultants miss the mark. They focus on preventing bad outcomes instead of identifying what creates them. Incorrect stakeholder analysis, misleading financial projections, defaced public communications—these aren't just operational risks, they're reputation destroyers that can end consulting relationships permanently.</p>

                <h3>Question 3: What happens if the data/service is missing or unreachable?</h3>
                
                <p>In my Treasury work, this question revealed critical dependencies that could halt billion-pound programs. Missing key stakeholder buy-in, unreachable subject matter experts, deleted critical documentation—these gaps compound rapidly in complex projects.</p>

            <section class="content-section" id="section-4">
                <h2>The Probability-Impact Matrix: Your 5-Minute Risk Prioritizer</h2>
                <img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-dFqh6BoA_x/b7eeaa8dbcd6e3e8fcf063219b4d4cb2193da75c9e8ee243be03b26a55df09e24998198a1ee2efbb425f017d43c6e66910d563ba98977f6ad551f7a2a69395b4119c92c4b7f4e1bda1cd7200a75beb9b76ad2a8d4150cdb6ff88b884f689faeea443e0ed" alt="Risk Assessment Matrix Grid" class="section-image">
                
                <p>While academic risk management courses spend weeks on complex probability calculations, I've learned to use a simple 5x5 matrix that delivers immediate clarity for decision-makers. This tool has saved me countless hours and positioned me as the consultant who cuts through analysis paralysis.</p>

                <h3><img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-GgQahPdmd1/9d61c9650b1efe06c075d4c16bfe7f929782b8d10692df517c2aaf2cc63691207ee3b655d077204e152f53c47e515cbeaa88fdbaab853c886eef24be3fea269af7d8e0dfe546604bc5dbf8cf201959ea53afb16c2bce1460478136267e1e128e0ff42738" class="icon-inline"> Probability Scoring (1-5 Scale):</h3>
                
                <ul style="margin: 1rem 0; padding-left: 2rem;">
                    <li><strong>1 (Very unlikely):</strong> Less than 10% chance - might never happen in project timeframe</li>
                    <li><strong>2 (Not likely):</strong> 10-30% chance - possible but not probable</li>
                    <li><strong>3 (Possible):</strong> 30-50% chance - could go either way</li>
                    <li><strong>4 (Probable):</strong> 50-80% chance - likely to occur without intervention</li>
                    <li><strong>5 (Very likely):</strong> 80%+ chance - assume it will happen</li>
                </ul>

                <h3><img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-jgVS4xTgx3/2224d166a96b80b9f9645f498b0e4a3b711022602d1947d48c7c239e6ac8a30360acbc622031938acb697d4a37ceda529609f4c080136da2b502626fe62e0b2c2d7ee62230f7ecd78cb31b6f9a0ba7bf8a2de50572186292e4d01e4394924dcf4d1000ac" class="icon-inline"> Impact Scoring (1-5 Scale):</h3>
                
                <ul style="margin: 1rem 0; padding-left: 2rem;">
                    <li><strong>1 (Negligible):</strong> Minor inconvenience, easily absorbed</li>
                    <li><strong>2 (Low):</strong> Manageable consequences, standard contingency handling</li>
                    <li><strong>3 (Moderate):</strong> Significant effort required, timeline impact possible</li>
                    <li><strong>4 (Significant):</strong> Major consequences, substantial recovery time</li>
                    <li><strong>5 (Severe):</strong> Project-threatening, relationship-damaging outcomes</li>
                </ul>

                <p>The magic happens when you multiply these scores. Anything scoring 13-25 demands immediate attention. Scores of 7-12 need monitoring and contingency planning. Scores of 1-6 can be documented but don't require active management.</p>

                <div class="check-in">
                    <h4><img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-1axrFdTims/828a9aa2bf6812a141305129f35d8b47e2faf102904867d01b7e47ed8b39d5f289b5bda0e5757f15d8406de6c2d6970ff4ef344ae661a7d6afcc1b3a47d65ebcd5bd715fada070e4e2c74240ca306554aec795d387a655aeedf6c73f6fee2ccfe0bbecff" class="icon-inline"> Risk Calculation Exercise</h4>
                    <div class="question">A key stakeholder who controls budget approval is likely to leave the organization during your project (Probability: 4). If they leave, the project will be delayed by 3 months and require finding a new sponsor (Impact: 4). What's the risk score and priority level?</div>
                    <div class="options">
                        <div class="option" data-correct="false">Score: 8 (Medium priority - monitor and plan)</div>
                        <div class="option" data-correct="true">Score: 16 (High priority - immediate attention required)</div>
                        <div class="option" data-correct="false">Score: 12 (Medium priority - monitor and plan)</div>
                        <div class="option" data-correct="false">Score: 20 (High priority - immediate attention required)</div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="feedback">
                        <strong>Perfect calculation!</strong> Probability 4 × Impact 4 = 16, which falls in the 13-25 range requiring immediate attention. This is exactly the type of stakeholder risk that can derail projects and makes rapid identification so valuable.
                    </div>
                </div>

                <p>Here's what makes this powerful for premium consultants: you can complete this analysis in real-time during stakeholder meetings. While others are taking notes, you're providing immediate strategic insights that demonstrate your value.</p>

                <h2>Advanced Quantitative Techniques for High-Stakes Projects</h2>
                <img src="https://codahosted.io/docs/LS8jtbvEXB/blobs/bl-1IpYLkxmyN/14c4224206a67eb81606902c135583af48421cb351769c9638333502582bc4328b9247b68a937144f012ca57640fe1d015c1935ceb96f32fa644ec50e33f5334e967f4e0ab6413149615621d6f517f9ac6136051ab32f691ac5cbcabe70acaa2ef886c0d" alt="High-Stakes Project Environment Scene" class="section-image">
                
                <p>When I'm working on £billion+ government programs, stakeholders need more than qualitative assessments. They need quantitative risk analysis that can inform budget decisions and timeline planning. Here's the framework I use for high-value engagements.</p>

                <h3>Three-Point Estimation for Time and Cost Risks:</h3>
                
                <p>For each major risk, I estimate optimistic, most likely, and pessimistic outcomes. Using a weighted formula (Optimistic + 4×Most Likely + Pessimistic) ÷ 6, this provides realistic planning scenarios that account for uncertainty.</p>

                <p>For example, when assessing stakeholder engagement risks on a digital transformation project, I might estimate: optimistic scenario (2-week delay, £10k additional cost), most likely scenario (6-week delay, £50k additional cost), pessimistic scenario (16-week delay, £200k additional cost). The weighted calculation provides a realistic planning baseline.</p>

                <div class="check-in">

                    <div class="question">Using the three-point estimation formula, if a risk has Optimistic: 1 week delay, Most Likely: 4 weeks delay, Pessimistic: 10 weeks delay, what's the weighted estimate?</div>
                    <div class="options">

                const finalScore = Math.round(finalAssessmentScores.reduce((a, b) => a + b, 0) / finalAssessmentScores.length);
                
                document.getElementById('finalResults').style.display = 'block';
                document.getElementById('finalScore').textContent = finalScore + '%';
                
                let feedback = '';
                if (finalScore >= 90) {
                    feedback = '🎉 Outstanding mastery! You\'re ready to command premium rates with rapid risk assessment expertise.';
                } else if (finalScore >= 80) {
                    feedback = '✅ Strong performance! You have solid foundations for implementing rapid risk assessment techniques.';
                } else if (finalScore >= 70) {
                    feedback = '📚 Good progress! Review the lesson materials and practice the frameworks to strengthen your mastery.';
                } else {
                    feedback = '🔄 Consider reviewing the lesson content and retaking the assessment to improve your understanding.';
                }
                
                document.getElementById('finalFeedback').textContent = feedback;
            }
        }

        function exportResults() {
            const results = {
                lesson: 'Rapid Risk Assessment Techniques',
                overallMastery: document.getElementById('masteryScore').textContent,
                finalScore: document.getElementById('finalScore').textContent,
                completedQuestions: masteryScores.length + finalAssessmentScores.length,
                timestamp: new Date().toLocaleString()
            };
            
            const dataStr = JSON.stringify(results, null, 2);
            const dataBlob = new Blob([dataStr], {type: 'application/json'});
            
            const link = document.createElement('a');
            link.href = URL.createObjectURL(dataBlob);
            link.download = 'risk-assessment-lesson-results.json';
            link.click();
        }

        function restartLesson() {
            window.scrollTo(0, 0);
            masteryScores = [];
            finalAssessmentScores = [];
            questionResponses = {};
            
            // Reset all interactive elements
            document.querySelectorAll('.option').forEach(opt => {
                opt.classList.remove('selected', 'correct', 'incorrect');
            });
            
            document.querySelectorAll('.feedback').forEach(feedback => {
                feedback.style.display = 'none';
            });
            
            document.getElementById('finalAssessment').style.display = 'none';
            document.getElementById('finalResults').style.display = 'none';
            document.getElementById('proceedBtn').disabled = true;
            document.getElementById('proceedBtn').textContent = 'Continue to Final Assessment';
            
            updateMasteryScore();
        }
