The Trade Don't Cave Principle

Here's what separates premium consultants from commodity providers: never give concessions for free. If a client needs a lower price, you modify the scope, timeline, or deliverables accordingly. This principle has protected my rate integrity across every negotiation I've handled.

When the Cabinet Office requested a budget adjustment on a transformation project, my response wasn't to cut my daily rate. Instead, I presented three options: maintain the current scope at the current investment, reduce deliverables to fit their budget, or extend the timeline to spread costs across multiple budget periods. They chose to maintain the scope because they understood the value equation.

The "Trade Don't Cave" principle works because it reinforces your position as a value creator, not a cost center. Clients respect consultants who protect their rates because it signals confidence in their worth.
