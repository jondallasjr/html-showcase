My father and I each have freckles. He has red hair. Strong like a mighty oak. I was a boy - 13 I think. I had dyed blond hair. I was a little chunky/overweight and a little awkward.

The Adventure Begins

For Summer Camp my troop went to New River, in the virginias. My dad chaperoned and we signed up for the New River Adventure - a special program for just some of the boys to do.
Two freckled hands, one large and weathered, one small and eager, both gripping paddles with the same determination. Like father, like son.

Into the Rapids

White water rafting. Gosh it was so much fun to be so close to death. (Were we really? ... I think we really were, that if we messed up we could have capsized and/or drowned).
We took breaks where the river split and calmed down. It was so beautiful back there. We went down that little patch of river on our backs again and again, laughing with friends.

When Things Went Wrong

The trail got wetter and wetter, muddier and muddier. Each step became more treacherous.
I slipped and sliced my wrist open on a rock. I turned around, and called out to the boy behind me, "Help.. I'm bleeding", but it came out more as a factual statement than a desperate plea. It was meaty. Jagged. Muddy, dirty.

The Rescue

We have a battalion chief (squat, with a big belly, but strong, and with a thick mustache) in our boy scout troop. He cleaned off my gash with water. We pulled the duct tape off the sides of the raft - the duct tape was being used to number the boats. We wrapped it around my wrist.
We saw the van above the bridge way up in the mountains while we were still rafting from down below. It was parked in the middle of the bridge so we could spot it.

Weeks Later...

A few weeks after we returned from Summer Camp, Weird Al was playing at the 9:30 Club in Washington D.C. My dad and I went with Carl and Carlson. Carlson was one of my best friends in the boy scouts. His father Carl was real big and fat with a short beard, white hair, glasses, and german.

The 9:30 Club

We were on the second floor of the 9:30 club. Looking down on the band, but not very far away, still quite close. Black brick walls, blue and white lights. Weird Al playing the accordion on stage with a band, his long curly hair.

The Moment of Hope

At the concert, Weird Al asked if anyone from the audience wanted to request a song. Everyone started raising their hands. Mr. Carl told me I should raise my hand, because of my wrist, my cast. Then he grabbed onto my arm and raised my wrist cast in the air, waving it around. But then Weird Al chose one of his band members to choose the next song. What a funny letdown.

The Meet and Greet

After the show, which ended around midnight, Carlson and I wanted to stay to meet Weird Al. We waited a long time, maybe another hour or so. And then he came out! Sat down right in front of us. So kind and jolly and sweet. Carlson told Weird Al that he liked his shoes. Weird Al looked at his shoes, then thought for a moment and said "The... shoe store".

The Journey Home

On our way home (just me and my dad), I must have fallen asleep in the passenger seat. My dad was driving. It was late. And he normally wakes up at 5am. He was so tired. And, as family lore has it, he chanted to himself "I. WILL. NOT. HURT. THIS. BOY." And used that intention as his strength to get home. He stopped at a 7-11 for a coffee.

Some Adventures Change Everything

Some memories become treasures. The cast came off, but the music never stopped playing. Dad's freckles, my freckles, Weird Al's smile, and that purple SUV carrying us safely through the night.
