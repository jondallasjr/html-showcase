Going to See Weird Al with My Dad

A Memory in Norman Rockwell Style

"My father and I each have freckles. He has red hair. Strong like a mighty oak. I was a boy - 13 I think. I had dyed blond hair. I was a little chunky/overweight and a little awkward."
WHOOSH!
🚣‍♂️ The New River Adventure
OWW!
"It was so beautiful back there. There was a trail the guides showed us that went up river about 200m. Then you could float on your back back down the river to our resting spot. We went down that little patch of river on our backs again and again and again."
"We need to stop this bleeding!"
"I slipped and sliced my wrist open on a rock. I turned around, and called out to the boy behind me, 'Help.. I'm bleeding', but it came out more as a factual statement than a desperate plea."
⏰ A Few Weeks Later...
🎵 The 9:30 Club
"We were on the second floor of the 9:30 club. Looking down on the band, but not very far away, still quite close. Black brick walls, blue and white lights. Weird Al playing the accordion on stage with a band, his long curly hair."
"Anyone want to request a song?"
🤝 Meeting Weird Al
"I like your shoes!"
"The... shoe store."
"Carlson told weird Al that he liked his shoes. Weird Al looked at his shoes, then thought for a moment and said 'The... shoe store'."
🚗 The Drive Home
"It was late. And he normally wakes up at 5am. He was so tired. And, as family lore has it, he chanted to himself 'I. WILL. NOT. HURT. THIS. BOY.' And used that intention as his strength to get home."

A memory preserved in Norman Rockwell warmth,
of adventure, music, and a father's love.
