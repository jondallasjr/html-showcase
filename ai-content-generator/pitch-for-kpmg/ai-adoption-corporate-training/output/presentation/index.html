The Algorithm That Cut Off Care

  • 200 million patients scored annually by a healthcare algorithm used by major US hospitals
  • Used healthcare costs as a proxy for health needs
  • Problem: US healthcare system historically spends less on Black patients — even when equally sick
  • Result: Algorithm concluded Black patients were healthier than they actually were
  • Before fix: 17.7% of flagged patients were Black. After: 46.5%
  • The fix was simple: use direct health indicators instead of cost
"This wasn't a malicious programmer. It was a reasonable-sounding business decision that encoded decades of inequality into a system running at scale."
Adapted from Obermeyer et al., Science (2019)

What Is AI Bias?

  • Definition: When an AI system produces systematically unfair outcomes that privilege certain groups over others
  • Not a bug in the code — a failure in system design
  • AI doesn't create bias. It scales existing bias at machine speed
  • Trained on historical data = trained to replicate historical discrimination
  • Proxy discrimination is the #1 mechanism — algorithms rarely use race directly but use correlated variables
Part II

Where AI Bias Hides

Data Bias

  • Historical bias: Amazon's AI recruiting tool trained on 10 years of mostly-male resumes — penalized "women's" and all-women's colleges
  • Representation bias: Facial recognition: 34% error for darker-skinned women vs. 1% for light-skinned men (Gender Shades)
  • Measurement bias: Healthcare costs ≠ healthcare needs. The proxy assumption baked inequality into the model
  • Skin cancer AI: 106,950 training images. Only 10 showed brown skin. 1 showed dark skin.

Algorithmic & Evaluation Bias

  • Optimization bias: Facebook showed tech jobs to men even with neutral targeting — algorithm optimized for engagement
  • Feedback loops: Spotify recommended more male artists → users engaged → system became more male-skewed
  • Amplification: DALL-E generated "CEO" as white men 97% of the time
  • Metric blindness: Apple Card gave women lower credit limits. Overall accuracy looked fine — nobody measured gender fairness

When Bias Hits the Bottom Line

Case: Mobley v. Workday (2025)

  • Black applicant, over 40, with disability applied to 80–100 positions via Workday's AI screening
  • Rejected by nearly all without an interview — despite being qualified
  • First major class-action to establish AI vendor liability under anti-discrimination law
  • Landmark: AI vendors can be held liable, not just employers using the tool
Key precedent
If you build it, you own the bias

Case: Algorithmic Mortgage Bias

  • Black applicants needed credit scores ~120 points higher for the same approval rate (Lehigh University study)
  • Fintech algorithms charged Black/Latinx borrowers ~5 basis points more in interest (UC Berkeley)
  • CFPB now requires specific denial reasons from AI lending systems
  • The fix: instructing the AI to "use no bias" nearly eliminated racial disparities

Part IV

The Monday Morning Playbook

Six Steps Starting This Week

  • 1. Audit your data — Check representativeness. Identify proxy variables (ZIP code, education) that correlate with protected characteristics
  • 2. Measure fairness — Apply the 80% rule: selection rate for any group ≥ 80% of the highest group
  • 3. Build diverse teams — Include ethicists, social scientists, domain experts. Affected communities identify bias first
  • 4. Maintain human oversight — High-stakes decisions should never be fully automated
  • 5. Monitor continuously — Automated alerts when fairness metrics drift. Don't wait for annual reviews
  • 6. Get independent audits — Internal teams have blind spots. EU AI Act requires this for high-risk AI

Tools That Make It Measurable

  • IBM AI Fairness 360 — 70+ fairness metrics, pre/in/post-processing mitigation (open source)
  • Microsoft Fairlearn — Fairness assessment, visualization, constraints for training (open source)
  • Google What-If Tool — Visual no-code model inspection and scenario testing (open source)
  • Commercial: Fiddler AI, Arthur AI, Truera — real-time production monitoring with bias alerts

The Regulatory Reality

  • EU AI Act: Fines up to €35M or 7% of global turnover. High-risk AI rules take effect August 2026
  • NYC Local Law 144: Annual bias audits for automated hiring tools (active now)
  • California: 4-year data retention for automated employment decisions
  • Colorado & Texas: Impact assessments and comprehensive AI frameworks (2026)
  • 1,100+ AI bills introduced across US states in 2025 alone
The window to get ahead of regulation is closing

Three Things to Remember

  • Bias hides in proxies. Removing protected characteristics isn't enough. ZIP codes, costs, and keywords reproduce the same discrimination
  • Vendors are liable. Courts are holding AI tool builders — not just deployers — responsible for discriminatory outcomes
  • Auditing works. In every major case, the bias was discoverable through demographic analysis before deployment. Nobody checked.
